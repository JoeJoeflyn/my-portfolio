# Sketch 004: Contact CTA

## Design Question
What should the closing contact/CTA section feel like? No contact section
exists today. This explores how to close the page in the brutalist system.

## How to View
open .planning/sketches/004-contact-cta/index.html

## Variants
- **A: Terminal Prompt** — A fake terminal that invites a message; type and "send" shows a success state. Continues the terminal.txt metaphor.
- **B: Big CTA Block** — Oversized "LET'S BUILD" headline + availability status + direct email + socials. Loud, confident closer.
- **C: Availability Card + Form** — A status card (open to work, response time, timezone) beside a compact contact form. Practical, structured.

## What to Look For
- Does the terminal prompt feel consistent with the hero, or redundant if the hero is already a terminal?
- Does the big CTA block read as confident or shouty?
- Does the form actually lower friction, or is email + socials enough?
- Which closes the page with the right energy &mdash; invitation vs. declaration?
